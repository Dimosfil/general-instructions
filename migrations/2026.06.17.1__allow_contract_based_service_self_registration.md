# Allow Contract-Based Service Self-Registration

## Purpose

Propagate the accepted startup rule that web-facing services may self-register
when their config-service record is missing, but only through the documented
config-service guide and contract.

This fixes the startup gap where a service with self-registration enabled could
not start because no record existed yet, while still preventing agents and
services from guessing ports, inventing payloads, or writing config-service
storage directly.

## Merge Rules

- Keep the live config-service lookup requirement before a web-facing service
  binds or reserves a port.
- If the service record exists, bind only the port recorded in config-service
  and use config-service records for neighboring service endpoints.
- If the service record is missing and the project-local self-registration flag
  is `on`, the service must read the config-service guide and contract, list
  current service records, choose a port that is both free on the local host and
  absent from config-service, bind it, verify its local health endpoint, and
  create or update its record through the documented config-service operation.
- If the service record is missing and self-registration is `off`, stop startup
  with a clear blocker.
- If config-service is unavailable or lacks a documented registration contract,
  stop startup with a clear blocker. Do not invent registration payloads, write
  directly to config-service storage, reuse stale local runtime config, or bind
  fallback ports.
- Config-service itself should expose `GET /agent/guide` and
  `GET /agent/contract` and document service listing, record reads, registration
  writes, updates, validation, conflict handling, and secret-free fields.

## Verification

- Confirm web-facing service startup guidance allows contract-based
  self-registration only when enabled.
- Confirm the config-service contract guidance includes read endpoints,
  guide/contract endpoints, and documented create/update operations.
- Confirm the rule still blocks startup when config-service is unreachable or
  registration is undocumented.

## Rollback

Restore the previous stricter requirement that every web-facing service must
already have a complete config-service record before binding a port.
